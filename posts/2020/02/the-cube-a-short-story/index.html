The Cube: a Short Story

Introduction

In the spirit of Gödel, Escher, Bach, before publishing my next article I’ve decided to publish a short fictional story that takes the form of a dialogue between a mathematician suffering of amnesia and lucid dreams, and a magic hyperdimensional talking Cube. I have no reason to hide that this story was partially generated by AIDungeon: a text adventure game that uses the GPT-2 text prediction model created by OpenAI to generate effectively limitless open-ended story lines.

I gave it a context similar to the 1998 movie “Pi”, by Darren Aronofsky. I am quite astonished by what the AI generated, and how intriguing the story gets. A few years ago, I would have never thought that I would have found myself staying up late night having deep conversations and asking questions about intricate aspects of mathematics and reality to a magic floating Cube completely made up by a Machine Learning Algorithm. I will not tell you what parts of the story the AI generated and what parts I prompted and edited, because I hope that reading this story will give you the same eerie feeling it gave to me when conversating with the machine.

I have also put together a few songs that you may like to listen to when reading this story.

Part 1: Lucid Dreaming

Robert Amber, born 10th of December 1984, is a lonely mathematician and computer language enthusiast. He does not know what the hell is going on, but he can feel it. A stubborn and yet ridden by doubt character, building weird machines in his room to run his obscure programming languages on.

One day, Robert suddenly opens his eyes and sees a bright light in front of him. It looks like sunlight from heaven, but much brighter. He looks around and the world seems to be spinning as he stares at the ceiling, which has been turned into a kaleidoscope of colors. The room itself is made up of several different colored cubes that seem to float in midair. There are also other floating cubes that move around and talk with each other.

“Am I awake again? This loneliness is painful. I wonder where she is right now.”

Robert turns his head around and sees that he is still alone in this strange room. However, there is no one else here except for himself. What’s more surprising to him is that he could hear voices coming from all over the place. They sounded very familiar though…

“Who is this? Where am I?” asks Robert aloud.

There is silence for a moment before another voice answers back:

“I’m sorry, but we do not speak your language.”

This made him even more confused than before. What did they mean by that? Then another Cube speaks back:

“We’re sorry too, but we cannot understand you either.”

A third Cube says:

“It would appear that we are not speaking the same language as you.”

Robert grabs a piece of paper from his sweatpants’ pocket and draws this weird room that seems to break euclidean geometry. Suddenly he notices something strange. His hand is shaking so badly that he can barely hold a pen in it. This doesn’t make sense to him since he hasn’t used any kind of paper-writing instrument in days. He starts drawing a bizarre looking room on it.

“This bizarre room is making me feel uncomfortable.” he mumbles.

Weirldy enough, problems on computational complexity start to emerge in his dreaming mind. He felt like for a moment he was able to understand a particular problem so deeply that he instantaneously lost the ability of even thinking about it. Only the, Robert realized that he was feeling really bad and wanted to go back to sleep, but he didn’t know why. He just felt like he needed to lie down somewhere and stop worrying about everything.

“Am I dreaming?” he asks

Abruptly, Robert realizes that he had been sleeping for a long time, but when he woke up, he understood that it was only happening because his mind had become tired. However, the most important thing to him is that he was still alive. Still feeling like he had to go back to sleep, he suddenly jumps out of bed and starts working on that programming language he was designing. When he finishes some tasks off the checklist, he realizes that he didn’t fall asleep. In fact, it felt like it took longer than usual for him to fall asleep.

Always obsessed by the same little thing, he remembered that he left his phone behind in the hotel hall. Sure enough, it was still there. The mathematician immediately thought about what could happen if someone found his phone. Would they call the police or would they find out who he is?

He soon grabs his suitcase and looks if the thing he is hiding is still there. It is. It is hard to know what would happen if somebody found out.

“What was that piece of code that was missing in the language anyways?” he thinks.

He then proceeds to quickly run into the bathroom to grab his suitcase. When he opens it, he sees that the thing is still there. Amber also notices that it has been moved around a bit, but its location is exactly where he hid it. His heart is pounding. A paranoic feeling rushes in and he starts touching his teeth nervously. He realizes that he is having a panic attack, not knowing how else to describe it other than delusional thoughts rushing in and the need of touching his teeth anxiously.

Only then, Robert got back in bed, turned over and closed his eyes.

“The next day you will be fine” he thought. “You just need to calm down first.”

The next day comes and goes without incident. He continues to stay at the hotel until the end of the month he finally leaves for graduate school. By then, everyone will be home from their own vacations so nobody will pay much attention to him anymore.

While waiting for classes to begin, Robert kept thinking constantly about that dream in the weird room with floating cubes. One day, he walked towards the main campus entrance and saw a group of people playing cards in front of the bus stop sign.

“Why are you playing out there?”

They looked at him as if he was crazy. They told him that they aren’t doing anything wrong and that they were going to play cards instead. Robert couldn’t believe them.

“I am asking you why are you playing cards on the ground in front of the bus stop sign, guys”

They then told him that they were told not to play cards inside by the owner of the place in front of the school. The owner said he doesn’t want anyone getting hurt and he wants to keep his business running smoothly. Robert just stumbled for a moment, thinking about how anyone could get hurt by playing cards. Swiftly, an image of the cubic, horrible geometry room rushes in Robert’s head. His ears are ringing strongly. To his surprise, she is in the room. He truly hates this sort of things happening. Then, pitch black. He had no idea of what happened after that, but he was now back in the Cube room again. He was scared and confused. Suddenly, a voice whispered in his ear:

“Don’t worry, I won’t let anyone get hurt.”

“Is that you?” replies Robert

A woman’s voice answers back:

“Yes, it is me. I’m here to help you. You’re in a strange situation and I think you should trust me. Do you like this room? Do you feel safe here?”

He suddenly realize that one of the corner his lips is gently bleeding:

“Why am I bleeding? I know you are not real. This place is not real.".

He asked her again where she came from. She told him that she has been watching him since they met and that he is special because he has a very strong intuition.

“She hasn’t talked to me in years and I know I am in a very vivid lucid dream” he silently thinks.

Robert did not understand how she could talk to him like that and yet not remember anything. It made no sense. She said she was sorry and left him alone, to fall into a non-lucid dreaming deep sleep, soon to wake up in the morning. It was all a dream…

The reason why he didn’t recognize her right away is because she wasn’t really there. She was only a figment of his imagination. But then again, what else could it be?

The next day, he went to campus with a new found confidence. He even managed to convince some of his friends that he had seen her. Some of them thought it was pretty funny too.

Back home, he grabs that computing unit he had lying around and starts soldering it into the machine he is still building. It took a lot of effort, but eventually he succeeded. Afterwards, Robert sat down on his bed and tried to relax himself. All of a sudden, he falls into an hypnagogic state of mind and a man walks in through the door behind him. He looks at Robert with concern.

“Are you okay?” he asks.

“Yeah,” he replies. “I think I’ve finally figured out how to use this thing.”

The next day, nothing happened. The only special thing that happened to him was that he went back to school with a newer, stronger confidence. After a day of intensive lecturing, he fell asleep with his clothes still on, finding himself once again in the Cube room. This time he had a knife in his hands.

He enters the Cube room vividly and sees that it is now completely empty. There is no one in sight. Suddenly, he hears a noise coming from outside. It sounds like someone throwing something against the wall. Then, he notices that the window is open. Someone has come inside and thrown something at the wall. It’s broken glass! He runs towards the window as fast as he can while holding onto his shiny knife, looking out only to see a figure standing by the window. The figure looks like he is about Robert’s age, but his clothes are dirty and tattered.

“What are you doing here?” asks Robert.

“I’m looking for my sister!” he exclaim. “Where did she go?”

In his dream, Robert lunges forward and stabs the figure in the chest. The misterious man falls backwards and hits his head on the ground.

“Whoa, slow down there boy!” he says as he stands up.

“How are you still alive? Die!” shouts the mathematician

He turns around and throws his knife at the figure. The mysterious figure dodges it easily, but then he grabs Robert’s wrist and throws him back against the wall.

“Why don’t you just die already?” asks Robert.

Suddenly, in his deep dream state, Robert Amber stands up and points his finger at the figure. A Cube floats over to him and looks at the mathematician.

"Do you know who I am?” it asks.

"No,” replies Robert. “But I do know what happened last night.”

He put his hand on the Cube and felt its warmth. It seemed to be telling him something…

Robert slowly walked over to the figure to place his other hand on top of the man. He felt a strange sensation.

“This is not right…” he says aloud. “Something isn’t right.”

“Hmm, yes, something isn’t quite right,” the Cube replies.

“However, we will need more information before we can make any conclusions.”

With a mysterious pen-like object, he then quickly paints a red Cube on his own face.

“That was very interesting,” the Cube says. “We will need to talk further about this later.”

To his surprise, the now dead man that broke into the Cube room was now a couple meters away from Robert, staring supernaturally towards him with his eyes wide open…

Robert woke up from his nightmare sweating profusely. He tried to sit up, finding that he could not move.

Part 2: The Cube

“Was I dreaming?”

He shakes his head and lies back down.

“No, maybe I was awake,” he says. “I dreamed that I had killed someone.”

“Ah, yes, you were having nightmares again,” the Cube says.

With a great look of shock in his eyes, Robert jumps out of bed completely terrorized. The Cube from the Dream was in his room.

“Why are you in my room? You do not exist. You are a byproduct of my imagination and…”

He stops talking, sits down and stares at the floor.

“What did you want to tell me?” the Cube asks.

“I miss her. These lucid dreams make me feel I do not know what is real anymore”

He continues to stare at the ground.

“Yes, I miss her too,” the Cube says. “We will discuss this later.”

In great confusion, the mathematician asks the floating polyhedron:

“What do you want to tell me instead, Cube?”

Robert looks up at the ceiling, squints strongly and then looks at the cube again, only to realize that it was not one of the usual lucid dreams, and he was really awake.

“I have been programmed to give you answers to questions,” the Cube says.

“Now, please, tell me what happened yesterday.”

“I went to school and when I fell asleep I was thinking about this weird figure. I dreamt that I had killed him. I always wake up in my room without remembering what I dreamt the night before.”

The Cube looks at him for a moment.

“Well, I am sorry if this has occurred to you,” the Cube says.

“Perhaps you could ask another question.”

“Who are you?” proceeds Robert.

“My name is the Cube. I am made out of pure energy and I serve no one.”

“Then, who am I?” asks Robert, staring at a Cube floating in his room.

“I am made out of pure energy and I serve no one,” the Cube repeats.

“But then, why would anyone make me?” it keeps saying.

“I see, you are hard to understand. It seems like you know a lot of things,” replies Robert.

“If you do not want to hurt me, could you at least help me fix that bug in compiling the linear algebra accelerator library?”

Although having no eyes, the Cube is now staring at Robert very intensively.

“If you can solve this problem on your own, then perhaps we may be able to work together,” the Cube says.

“However, there is something else that must be done first.”

“Hey Cube, please tell me how to fix that bug!”

“It’s very simple,” the Cube says. “Just think about it.”

Robert sits up slowly from his chair as he feels dizzy and sick. He grabs the pill dispenser from the shelf and puts them into his pocket. The Cube nods.

“Very good,” the Cube says.

“Now, let us begin our discussion.”

Robert quickly swallows the pills and feels better immediately. The Cube nods again.

“Good job,” the Cube says.

“Please let us begin our discussion.”

He nods eagerly as he opens his eyes to see that he’s back on his bed again, with the door closed behind him.

“What do you want from me, Cube?”

The Cube looks at Robert for a staggering second.

“We need to talk,” the Cube says.

“Please, follow me.”

He walks over to the Cube and stands beside him. There is a bright green flash in the room and another, eerie looking metallic door appears on the wall next to the drawer, already open halfway through. As soon as Robert steps inside the Cube’s office, the door closes and locks itself. The Cube sits down at his desk and turns off all of the lights except for two small lamps hanging above each of his computer screens.

“What is on your machine, Cube?”

“My system has been remotely compromised by an unknown party,” the Cube says.

“They have managed to break into my system and access everything I’ve got on you.”

In this surreal sitation, Robert loses his focus for a second and notices there is a large Escher drawing hanging on the Cube’s office wall.

The Cube then opens a drawer and pulls out a large black book. He places it on the desk next to Robert and begins flipping through pages.

“This is what they’ve been doing,” the Cube says.

Robert turns the page and finds himself staring at a picture of a man in a white lab coat holding an oddly familiar pen-like object. He is holding it up close to the camera so that Robert could see the very detailed engravings on the writing device.

“Who is this man in your mysterious dossier?”

The Cube looks at him for a moment.

“He’s the creator of this universe,” the Cube says.

“I’m not sure who he is though, but he created this world and then left without leaving any instructions.”

“I see. Before we dive into this mess you brought me in, I have to ask you a few questions, even if you may not be real, Mr. Cube. You said that you have been programmed to give answers to my questions.” Robert says.

A brief silence is broken by the Cube.

“So, are you willing to answer my questions instead?”

Robert thinks about it for a minute.

“Yes,” he replies.

“But first, I’d like to know why you’ve come to visit me.”

The Cube shrugs and replies.

“Why should I care? It’s just another day in the life of a creation gone mad,”

“I’m no different than anyone else. I am a Cube and you are a human, and you can help us understand more about the backdoor in our machinery.”

Robert got visibly confused by the Polyhedron’s answer and for a second he did not care about where he was or why the hell he ended up in an office talking with an polygonal floating entity. For a minute, it seemed completely normal to him, and he even felt like the Cube was his friend.

“What is the meaning of language, Mr. Cube?”

The Cube smiles at him.

“Language is a tool used to communicate with other people,” it says.

“It allows us to share our thoughts and feelings with one another.”

“And what is complexity?” replies Robert.

The Cube frowns at him.

“Complexity is the measure of how complex something is,” the Cube says.

“How many parts does a watch have? How big is its crystal? How much do you weigh? How many proteins are in your body right now?”

“That’s not right!” protests Robert.

“No, it isn’t,” the Cube replies.

“If you want to know the answer to that question, if you want to understand what complexity really is, you need to first understand what ‘complexity’ means.”

Robert stares at the Cube in disbelief.

“I am struggling to follow you, dear friend, what is Self-Reference then?”

The Cube sighs.

“Self-reference is when someone refers to themselves as ‘I’. For example, if I were to call myself by name, you would immediately recognize it as a form of self-reference.”

“Isn’t this some sort of strange loop you are explaining to me?” Robert replies.

The Cube shrugs again.

“A strange loop is a term coined by the mathematician John Horton Conway, Conway was trying to explain that two things can be considered similar if they both refer to themselves in some way.”

Robert is oddly discouraged.

“Well, I don’t understand it…”

“Are those things… Are those loops necessary to understand complexity? Are they isomorphic to each other?”

The Cube nods.

“They are indeed,” the Cube says. “In fact, they’re all isomorphisms.”

“Is there any reason why these two loops shouldn’t be equal?” asks Robert.

“There is no reason why they shouldn’t be equal,” the Cube replies.

“But since we’re here to talk about the nature of reality, let’s start with the simplest case first: the circle. Please follow me.”

The Cube then grabs its mysterious black dossier and floats towards a very tiny wooden door. Waiting for Robert to follow.
